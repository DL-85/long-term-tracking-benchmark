Long-term tracking in the wild: a benchmark

 

 

We introduce a new video benchmark to assess single-object tracking algorithms in the long-term scenario, where videos can last several minutes and present target object disappearance.
Benchmarks have enabled great strides in the field of object tracking by defining standardized evaluations on large sets of diverse videos. However, these works have focused exclusively on short sequences of a few tens of seconds, where the target object is always present. Consequently, most researchers have designed methods tailored to this "short-term" scenario, which is poorly representative of practitioners' needs.
Aiming to fill this gap, we curate and further annotate YouTube Bounding Boxes to compile a Long-Term, Large-Scale Tracking dataset of sequences with average length greater than two minutes and with frequent target disappearance. This dataset is the largest ever for single object tracking: it comprises 366 sequences for a total of 1.5 millions frames, 25 times more than the popular OTB-100.
